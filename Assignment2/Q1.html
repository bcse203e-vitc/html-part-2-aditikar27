Why is It Important to Achieve Gender Equality

Aditi Kar

Published on : 15 December , 2024

It is important to recognize that there is no single definition of gender and that it varies from culture to culture.
However, in general, gender refers to the social and cultural roles that are assigned to people based on their sex.
These roles can vary greatly from person to person but they should not be viewed as limiting.
Unfortunately, in patriarchal societies, men are entitled to have more power, money, and several other privileges than women.
The differences in power dynamics between the two genders have kept women away from the decision-making process and have oppressed them in many ways.
A society where people experience oppression, violence, and discrimination because of their gender is not sustainable and can not achieve true prosperity.
Without equality, there is no justice. Without justice, there is no peace.

We must also acknowledge that gender-based discrimination faced by women is not the same for everybody.
This is an intersectional issue. The life experience of a woman depends on her race, ethnicity, socioeconomic class, and physical ability.
For instance, the experience of oppression faced by a privileged, upper-class, white woman in the USA is not comparable to the oppression
and discrimination faced by a differently abled, poor woman from a racially marginalized community.
Unfortunately, many people still believe that there are differences between the genders.
But regardless of the differences, we should aim to create a society where everyone feels safe, and everyone has equal access and rights to opportunity.
